This kind of puzzle is called a nonogram.

Click on a square to change it from white to black, or vice versa.

The numbers at the edges of the grid tell you how many squares should be colored black in each row and column.

For example, the 4 on top of the third column means that there should be 4 consecutive black squares in that column.

The 2 and 1 on top of the second column mean that there should be 2 consecutive black squares, a space (or spaces), and then a single black square.
